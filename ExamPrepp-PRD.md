1. Executive Summary  ExamPrepp is an AI-powered web application that helps Nigerian university students prepare smarter for exams. Students upload their past question papers, and the AI analyzes historical question patterns to predict the topics most likely to appear in their next exam. They then upload their lecture notes, and ExamPrepp generates a comprehensive, grounded study guide focused exclusively on those high-probability topics — using only the student's own materials as the source of truth.  ONE-LINE PITCH  Stop guessing what to study. Upload your past papers and notes. ExamPrepp tells you  what's coming — then teaches you exactly that.  2. Problem Statement  2.1 The Student Pain  Nigerian university students face a structurally difficult exam preparation problem. Course syllabuses are broad, lecturer notes are dense, and exam time is short. The dominant study strategy — reading everything and hoping — produces anxiety and poor results. The students who consistently outperform their peers do something different: they manually mine years of past question papers to identify recurring patterns, then focus their reading on those high-probability areas. This process, done well, takes 3–5 days.  Most students either don't know this technique exists, don't have access to enough past papers to make it work, or run out of time before they can complete it. The knowledge of "how to study smarter" is unevenly distributed — students with older siblings, good senior friends, or money for tutorials get it. Students without those advantages don't.  VALIDATED BY THE FOUNDER  ExamPrepp's core concept was validated through direct personal experience: the PM used  Claude directly for exam prep and found that AI-assisted pattern analysis of past questions  was genuinely effective for predicting exam topics. The tool is built from a real workflow that  worked.  2.2 The Market Context  FUTO (Federal University of Technology Owerri) is the initial target institution, with a student population of approximately 30,000. Nigerian university enrollment exceeds 1.8 million students across 170+ institutions. Every one of these students faces an exam every semester. The "past question economy" is already massive in Nigeria — WhatsApp groups, Telegram channels, and

At the institutional level, this translates into avoidable failure rates. In the short term (Hack4FUTO MVP): a web app that proves the core AI loop works end-to-end — upload past papers, get predictions, upload notes, get a study guide.  In the medium term (post-hackathon v1): a full product with auth, persistent study sessions, a flashcard and quiz engine, and a crowdsourced past question repository that creates a flywheel of growth.  In the long term: institutional B2B partnerships with universities, multi-course dashboards, adaptive study plans, and eventually a continental expansion across Anglophone Africa.  4. 7.1 Core Flow — Phase 1: Past Question Analysis  As a   exam-season student , I want to   upload my past question papers and immediately  see which topics are most likely to appear on my exam , so that I can stop guessing what  to read and focus my limited study time on what actually matters.  ✓   System accepts PDF and image file uploads (no hard file limit).  ✓   System warns the student if fewer than 3 past question files are uploaded.  ✓   AI analyzes patterns across all uploaded files and returns 5–7 ranked topics.  ✓   Each topic shows a probability score (50–95%) and a one-sentence reason grounded in the  papers.  ✓   Analysis completes and displays within 60 seconds of upload.  As a   student uploading only 1 past question paper , I want to   be warned that my results  will be less accurate , so that I set the right expectations for the output quality and know to  find more papers before relying on the predictions.  ✓   System displays a visible quality warning: "Only 1 past question file uploaded. Add at least 3  years of past papers for reliable predictions."  ✓   System does NOT block the analysis — the student can proceed.  ✓   Warning is dismissible.  7.2 Core Flow — Phase 2: Study Guide Generation  As a   student who has seen their topic predictions , I want to   upload my lecture notes  and receive a study guide that explains exactly those predicted topics in clear  language , so that I get a study guide grounded in my lecturer's material rather than generic  internet content, and I can trust its accuracy.  ✓   System prompts a second upload step after Phase 1 completes.  ✓   Student uploads lecture notes (PDFs/images, no file limit).  ✓   AI generates a markdown study guide covering all predicted topics.  ✓   Every explanation is grounded in the uploaded lecture notes.  ✓   If a predicted topic is absent from lecture notes, the guide explicitly flags it and covers it using the AI's general knowledge instead, clearly labeled as such, so no topic is left unexplained.  ✓   Guide is rendered inline and downloadable.  As a   student who has received their study guide , I want to   convert it into flashcards so  I can quiz myself on the key concepts , so that active recall helps me retain information  better than passive reading.  ✓   Flashcard generation button appears after study guide is rendered.  ✓   10–15 flashcards are generated from the guide content.  ✓   Cards are displayed in a tap-to-flip interface.  ✓   Student can cycle through all cards.
